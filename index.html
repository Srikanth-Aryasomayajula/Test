Static Analysis: Static analysis is the foundation of structural analysis. It's employed when the structure is in equilibrium and subjected to a constant load or force.

  • Force, displacement, velocity, acceleration are not functions of time. Steady-state conditions, with no consideration of time.
  • The assumption is that materials and loads do not change with time.
  • Suitable for structures subjected to slow, constant, or quasi-static loading conditions.
  • Governing equation is [K]{x} = {F}, where [K] is the stiffness matrix, {x} and {F} are the displacement and force vectors.
  • Less solution time
  • Output: stress, displacement
  • Inertial loads (due to g) are not considered.
  • Only the stiffness matrix is solved.
  • Example: A person sitting on a chair.

Dynamic Analysis: It deals with the time-dependent behavior of structures. This analysis considers the effects of forces and displacements as they change over time.

  • Force, displacement, velocity, acceleration are functions of time.
  • Governing equation: [M]{ẍ} + [C]{ẋ} + [K]{x} = {F}, where [M] is the mass matrix, [C] is the damping matrix, and {ẍ}, {ẋ} are acceleration and velocity vectors.
  • More solution time
  • Output: stress, displacement, velocity, acceleration w.r.t. time/frequency

Free Vibration: Displacement, velocity, acceleration are functions of time but there’s no external force.
Example: A mass is oscillating freely in a spring mass system.

Forced Vibration: External force is also present

  • Inertial loads are also taken into consideration.
  • Stiffness matrix is solved along with mass matrix (and damping matrix, if not zero).
  • Example: Load applied on a spring.
